# TIØ4145 — Corporate Finance

## Overview
TIØ4145 Corporate Finance focuses on modern financial theory and its application to real-world business decision making. The course covers portfolio theory, investment analysis, bond pricing, asset pricing models such as CAPM and APT, option pricing, capital structure decisions, dividend policy, risk management, and sustainable finance. Students learn how companies evaluate investments, finance operations, and manage financial risk using both theoretical models and practical tools such as spreadsheet modelling and sensitivity analysis. The course is particularly relevant for students interested in investments, finance, managerial accounting, and strategic corporate decision making. :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}

## Goals
- Develop a strong understanding of valuation methods for financial assets, companies, and real investments.
- Learn how financial theory supports investment, financing, and risk management decisions in practice.
- Gain experience with spreadsheet modelling, company valuation, and sensitivity analysis using real-world data.
- Understand key concepts related to capital structure, dividend policy, and sustainable finance. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

## Recommended prior knowledge
The course assumes familiarity with financial mathematics, present value calculations, investment analysis, microeconomics, and financial accounting. Knowledge equivalent to TIØ4118 Industrial Economic Analysis and TIØ4105 Business Economics is recommended. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

## Teaching approach / themes
The course combines lectures, exercises, practical assignments, and guest lectures from industry professionals. Students work with real financial data and spreadsheet-based analysis to apply theoretical concepts to practical managerial problems. A major recurring theme is the interaction between investment decisions, financing decisions, and risk. The course balances rigorous financial theory with hands-on analytical work and emphasizes both quantitative reasoning and practical application. Exercises are compulsory and must be approved to access the final exam. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}
