Format: 4-hour written digital exam delivered through Inspera. Four sections.
        Section 1 is multiple choice. Sections 2, 3 and 4 are written;
        students may answer those by hand on physical sheets that get
        scanned in (each sub-question has a seven-digit submission code).
        Question language: English.

Duration (hours): 4

Allowed aids: A specific basic (non-programmable) calculator, plus a
              formula sheet provided in Inspera. No other printed or
              hand-written material is allowed.
              [FORMULA SHEET — paste contents below when available]

Grading: A–F. Section weighting (per the 2024 paper):
         Section 1 (MCQ)      20%
         Section 2 (written)  30%
         Section 3 (written)  20%
         Section 4 (written)  30%
         MCQ scoring rule: +2 for a correct answer, −1 for a wrong answer,
         0 for blank. Students should therefore skip MCQs they cannot
         eliminate down to a 50/50.

Common question types:
- Section 1 (MCQ): conceptual single-answer questions, one per major
  course topic. Typical pattern is "Which of the following statements
  about X is FALSE?" or "Which best describes …". Past topics in MCQ have
  included: valuation of bonds (zero-coupon yields and the term
  structure), valuation of companies (DCF vs. normalized earnings),
  capital budgeting (NPV decisions, tax-loss carryforwards), debt markets
  (expected loss, callable bonds), financial options (delta, risk-neutral
  probabilities), the flow-to-equity method, sustainable finance, and
  real options.
- Sections 2–4 (written): multi-part numerical/derivation problems
  drawn from one major topic each. Sub-questions typically build on each
  other (a → b → c → d). Examples:
    * Bond duration and interest-rate sensitivity; expected growth and
      earnings under a stated payout/leverage policy.
    * Two-period binomial option pricing; risk-neutral valuation;
      American vs. European; effect of dividends; put–call parity.
    * Capital structure with corporate taxes: filling in tables for
      levered/unlevered firms using MM, WACC, cost of equity, value of
      tax shield.
    * Flow-to-equity / APV / WACC valuation of a project or firm given
      free cash flows and a target capital structure.
    * Portfolio choice and the CAPM: computing betas, expected returns,
      tangency-portfolio weights.

Historically tested topics (rough frequency across 2017–2024 papers):
- Bond pricing, YTM, duration, term structure — appears almost every year.
- Option pricing (binomial trees, risk-neutral valuation, put–call parity,
  Black–Scholes intuition, American vs. European, dividend effect) —
  appears almost every year, often as a large written section.
- Capital structure with taxes (MM I/II, WACC, value of tax shield,
  trade-off theory) — appears almost every year, often as a "fill in the
  table" written task.
- Valuation methods (DCF, APV, WACC, flow-to-equity, normalized
  earnings) — appears almost every year.
- CAPM and portfolio theory (beta, expected returns, efficient frontier,
  tangency portfolio) — recurring.
- Capital budgeting decision rules and side effects (tax-loss
  carryforwards, sensitivity analysis) — recurring.
- Real options (option to delay, abandon, expand) — recurring since 2021.
- Sustainable finance — recurring in the MCQ section since 2021.
- Payout policy (dividends vs. repurchases, irrelevance results) —
  occasional.
- Market efficiency / EMH — occasional, usually conceptual MCQ.

Special notes:
- The exam is delivered in Inspera. Students should practice working
  numerical problems digitally (no scratch in the printed paper) but can
  use physical handwritten sheets for derivations in sections 2–4.
- The MCQ negative-marking rule is unusual; emphasize it in study plans.
- Several years' papers come with a separate "*_solutions.pdf" and
  2021/2023/2024 have separate "*_mcq" and "*_mcq_solutions" files —
  these are the single best signal for the level of precision and
  notation expected on the written sections.
- Past exam questions tend to use Berk & DeMarzo notation directly, so
  any practice questions and worked examples should follow suit.
